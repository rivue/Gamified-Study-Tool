Automatic Course Generation Tool:
Core Idea: Build a platform where you can upload documents or other supporting materials (in
students case a 300 page textbook, in companies case maybe Rules, QnA, policies, something
like that) then build software which automatically extracts ideas from each chapter / sub chapter
and breaks them into modules with a quiz / lecture / maybe interactive part (the lectures would
be videos generated with ai) then there are quizzes at the end of each module, and a bigger test
for users at the end of each chapter, and would have lots of other stuff that users could learn
from (drag/drop, matching, small entertaining games, interaction with an Al avatar). Users
could upload these (the courses) and have other people use them, much like quizlet, and each
course could have a progress bar, not only telling users how long they are through the textbook,
but also tell users how many ideas they have covered, like you take one quiz at the end of the
chapter and it only covers 5/12 ideas or concepts in the chapter, but you take another and
another quiz until you have fully mastered all ideas in the textbook. Could be like Duolingo in
that the review / learning process could be entertaining / interactive and also possibly have a
concept map / network map of all the main ideas not completely unlike Doulingo. Similarly, as
you progress and advance more in the course, you could be asked harder and harder
questions, like free response questions that involve multiple things you've learned so far. This
would be a subscription based thing in order for users to have access to > 3 courses (haven't
decided on the exact number yet), (I would heavily discount / give it for free to students from my
school as a trial) but it would alleviate the need for college students to be taught by teachers
they can't understand, but it could also be useful for homeschooling or advertisement or safety
training videos, really anything in case that market doesn't work out the way I think it could.
Why people would use this product:
Students creating courses for the classes they are taking
Right now, creating flash cards / studying is boring and tedious. Creating flash
cards takes lots of valuable time that could be spent studying and on top of that,
they are stale and cannot be elaborated on. With our Al generated informative
videos, Doulingo-like learning, and ai companian, I (we) will make learning from a
boring material like a textbook fun and engaging. Additionally, there are lots of
productivity tools out there that attempt to gamify habits, but do they really? They
do not tie directly into reviewing course material and only provide a pretty ui over
a shit excuse for a basic ToDo list. My (our) product will gamify learning by taking
the content and make it fun and engaging for all learning styles. Students will use
our product because it has all of these things in one that these other tools lack
and will crave my (our) product because it turns their long tedious hours studying
into a fun game.
The main competitor I am worried about is turbolearn.ai, but I think our product
will be superior because all they do is turn lectures into notes and a couple
flashcards while we actually facilitate the learning process
