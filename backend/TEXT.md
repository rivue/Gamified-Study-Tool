# Summary of Culture and Interpersonal Communication ## Understanding Culture - **Definition**: Culture encompasses the language, values, beliefs, traditions, symbols, and customs shared and learned by a group of people. - **Key Concepts**: - **Co-Culture**: A smaller cultural group within a larger culture, often defined by shared characteristics (e.g., ethnicity, interests). - **Social Identity**: The way individuals identify themselves within the context of their cultural and social groups. ## Intercultural Communication - **Influence of Culture on Communication**: - **Low Context vs. High Context**: - *Low Context*: Communication relies heavily on explicit verbal messages (e.g., Western cultures). - *High Context*: Communication relies on implicit messages and context (e.g., Asian cultures). - **Collectivism vs. Individualism**: - *Collectivism*: Emphasis on group goals and relationships (e.g., Japan). - *Individualism*: Emphasis on personal goals and autonomy (e.g., USA). - **Power Distance**: - *High Power Distance*: Acceptance of unequal power distribution (e.g., India). - *Low Power Distance*: Preference for egalitarian relationships (e.g., Sweden). - **Uncertainty Avoidance**: - *High Uncertainty Avoidance*: Preference for clear rules and stability (e.g., Greece). - *Low Uncertainty Avoidance*: Comfort with ambiguity and change (e.g., Singapore). - **Achievement vs. Nurture**: - *Achievement*: Focus on success and accomplishments (e.g., USA). - *Nurture*: Emphasis on caring and quality of life (e.g., Sweden). ## Co-Cultures & Communication - **Intersectionality**: The interconnected nature of social categorizations such as: - **Race / Ethnicity** - **Gender / Sexual Orientation** - **Age / Generation** - **Socioeconomic Status** - **Disability** ### The Power of the Single Story - **Reflection Questions**: - How do we support or diminish Co-Cultural differences? - What influences your communication style regarding these differences? - Why do we often seek a "single story" perspective? - Recall a time when you recognized only a single story. What intersections were overlooked? ## Communication Codes - **Types of Codes**: - **Verbal Codes**: Language and verbal style. - *Example*: Code Switching – adapting language based on the audience. - **Nonverbal Codes**: Body language, gestures, and microaggressions. - *Example*: A smile can convey warmth but may also be misinterpreted in different cultures. ## Intercultural Communication Competence - **Essential Skills**: - **Motivation & Attitude**: Willingness to engage with different cultures. - **Tolerance for Ambiguity**: Comfort with uncertainty in communication. - **Open-mindedness**: Acceptance of diverse perspectives. - **Knowledge & Skill**: Understanding cultural nuances and communication styles. - **Patience & Perseverance**: Commitment to improving intercultural interactions. ### Mnemonic for Intercultural Communication Competence: **MOTOKP** - **M**otivation - **O**pen-mindedness - **T**olerance for ambiguity - **K**nowledge & Skill - **P**atience & Perseverance ## Practice Questions 1.
